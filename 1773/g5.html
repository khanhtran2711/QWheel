🟪 GROUP 5 — Healthcare.gov Launch

Platform: Desktop website

Your screenshot shows: Homepage and Login https://www.healthcare.gov/ https://www.healthcare.gov/login

The problem to fix: When the US government's health insurance website launched in 2013, users were forced to create a full account before they could see any insurance plans or prices. Most users had no idea what they were signing up for or whether the site was even relevant to them. Combined with technical crashes, almost no one could complete any task. This single design decision — registration before browsing — made the entire site fail at launch.

Your task: Redesign the entry flow so that users can explore plans and prices before being asked to register. Your redesign must still collect an account at the right moment — just not as the very first step.

Focus on: Onboarding, trust-building, and removing barriers

Your pitch must answer: "At what point in your redesign does the user need to create an account, and why at that moment specifically?"
